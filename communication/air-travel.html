PART 1C 🎙 Pronunciation

Connected Speech

Speak more naturally with these everyday reductions.

🔗 Reduction Rule: Could you / Would you

could you sounds like "couldja"
would you sounds like "wouldja"

In natural, relaxed English, "could you" and "would you" are often reduced when speaking. The letters blur together — this is called connected speech. You don't need to sound exactly like this, but you should be able to recognize and understand it!

Practice Sentences

Could you put your seat forward now, sir? We'll be landing shortly.
→ "Couldja put your seat forward..."
Could you fill out this customs form before we arrive, please?
Could I check your ticket, please?
I wonder if I could change my seat.
I can't see the TV screen very well from where I'm sitting.
Excuse me, I can't find the safety instruction card.
These headphones don't seem to be working properly.
I think there's a problem with my seat number.
I should be in business class, but this seat number is for a seat in economy class.
