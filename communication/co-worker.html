C. Pronunciation Focus

The three sounds of the "-ed" ending, and how it connects to the next word.

The -ed ending of regular past tense verbs has 3 different pronunciations, depending on the final sound of the base verb — not the final letter.

/ɪd/ after /t/ or /d/

Used when the verb ends in the sound /t/ or /d/.

e.g. invested, started

/t/ after voiceless sounds

Used when the verb ends in: /p/, /k/, /f/, /s/, /ʃ/, /tʃ/, /θ/.

e.g. worked, fixed

/d/ after voiced sounds & vowels

Used for all other cases: /b/, /g/, /v/, /z/, /m/, /n/, /l/, /r/... and every vowel sound.

e.g. managed, trained

🔗 Linking -ed to a vowel sound

When a word ending in -ed is followed by a word that starts with a vowel sound, native speakers link the ending sound (/t/, /d/, /ɪd/) to that vowel, making speech smoother and more natural.

A. Grammar Focus
Tense & Accents

To talk about past work experience, you need to clearly distinguish between these three tenses:

Past Simple
Explain Describes an action that is completely finished in the past.
Formula S + V2/ed
Example "I ran my own construction company."
Simple Example "I worked at a bank for five years."
Past Continuous
Explain Describes an action that was in progress, or continued around a specific point in the past.
Formula S + was/were + V-ing
Example "I was working 60 hours a week before I came here."
Simple Example "I was studying English when she called me."
Present Perfect
Explain Describes experience up to the present moment, without a specific time being important.
Formula S + have/has + V3/ed
Example "I have worked in a few different teams."
Simple Example "I have visited three countries this year."

Section 1 · Polite Requests & Desires

In interviews or on the first day of a new job, people often use Past Simple with stative verbs (want, wonder) to make a request or offer sound more polite and gentle.

"Did you want a tour of the office?"
"I wanted to ask about the company's history."

Part 4 · Discussion

Role Play: Meeting a New Colleague

Apply everything you've learned in a realistic conversation.

🤝Job Interview / Networking Event
You are at a job interview, or a networking event. A new colleague or interviewer asks: "What did you do before you came here?"
Goal: Prepare a short answer (3–5 sentences) describing your previous job.
work as / work in was in charge of responsible for Past Continuous for busy periods

Suggested flow:

  1. Say your old job title and department (Past Simple + work as / work in).
  2. Say your main responsibility (was in charge of / responsible for).
  3. Add an experience or how busy it was (Past Continuous, optional).
"Before I came here, I worked as an IT assistant. I was in charge of ensuring all technological systems were maintained. It was a tough job — I was working nearly 50 hours a week before I decided to look for a new environment."
Extension questions
  • What was the most interesting part of your previous job?
  • Why did you decide to change jobs or companies?
  • What skills from your old job do you still use today?
