The problem

[P] There has been an increase in air pollution in my city because of too many motorbikes. Traffic is terrible too — at rush hour it takes an hour and a half to cross the city. And garbage is everywhere: the landfills are full and there is no room for new ones. (Đang có sự gia tăng về ô nhiễm không khí ở thành phố tôi vì có quá nhiều xe máy. Giao thông cũng rất tệ. Và rác thải thì ở khắp nơi.)

The causes

Why? Mostly overpopulation. Too many people move here, so the city faces a serious housing shortage — and unemployment is a growing problem for young people. Factories increase the pollution when they dump chemicals into the river, and we import more packaged food every year, which creates even more waste. (Tại sao? Chủ yếu là do bùng nổ dân số. Quá nhiều người chuyển đến nên thành phố thiếu hụt nhà ở nghiêm trọng, và thất nghiệp là vấn đề nhức nhối với người trẻ.)

The effect

[E] If we don't stop this, we'll face health problems like coughing and lung disease. The environment will get dirty, tourists won't come, and the economy suffers. Global warming makes it worse — our summers are hotter and our winters are warmer every year. (Nếu không ngăn chặn, chúng ta sẽ gặp vấn đề sức khỏe như ho và bệnh phổi. Môi trường sẽ bẩn, khách du lịch không đến, và kinh tế bị ảnh hưởng.)

The solution

[R] The government really needs to help build more affordable houses and create more jobs. We have to do something about the waste we produce — I reduce my own garbage by buying fresh food instead of frozen dinners, and I bring my own cup to the coffee shop. Everyone can take action: use public transport, plant more trees, and recycle. (Chính phủ thực sự cần giúp xây thêm nhà giá rẻ và tạo thêm việc làm. Chúng ta phải làm gì đó về lượng rác thải mình tạo ra. Ai cũng có thể hành động.)

Part 2Pronunciation

Word Stress — Noun vs Verb

Same spelling. Different stress. Different meaning. Get this wrong and people hear the wrong word.

The Rule

First syllable = noun · Second syllable = verb

📌 For two-syllable words that work as BOTH a noun and a verb: stress the FIRST syllable for the noun, and the SECOND syllable for the verb.
🎯 REcord (N) · reCORD (V)

(Quy tắc: với từ hai âm tiết vừa là danh từ vừa là động từ — nhấn âm tiết ĐẦU khi là danh từ, nhấn âm tiết SAU khi là động từ.)
🥁 Think of it like a drum beat. The noun beats early — BOOM-ta. The verb beats late — ta-BOOM. Clap it while you say it: clap loud on the stressed part. Your mouth will follow your hands.
⚠️ Why it matters here: "There is an INcrease in pollution" (noun — a thing) vs "Factories inCREASE pollution" (verb — an action). Same letters, opposite job in the sentence.

Part 5Critical Thinking + Speaking

P-E-R Speaking

Problem → Effect → Resolution. Three moves, one minute, any issue in the world.

🩺 Think of it like a doctor's visit. The doctor names what's wrong (Problem), warns what happens if you ignore it (Effect), then writes the prescription (Resolution). Skip any step and nobody takes you seriously — a problem with no solution is just complaining.
P

Problem

State the issue clearly. What is happening? How bad is it?

"There has been an increase in…"
"… is a growing problem…"
E

Effect

What will happen if we do nothing? Give a clear consequence.

"If we don't stop this, we'll…"
"If people don't…, they…"
R

Resolution

What should we do about it? Give a practical solution.

"The government really needs to…"
"We have to do something about…"
