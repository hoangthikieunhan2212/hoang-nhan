Steve's Bad Week

Last week, Steve was scheduled to give a big presentation at work on Friday. At first, he felt great. However, on Sunday afternoon he felt sick. He had a headache, and his brain felt heavy after long hours on the computer. He also had a fever, a sore throat, a runny nose and a stuffy nose. He was sneezing all morning, and his body aches made it hard to sleep. "It seems like the flu," he thought.

So Steve decided to see a doctor. He took a day off and made an appointment at a clinic near his house. At the clinic, he had to register, get a number, and wait in line with the other patients. Then the nurse called him: "It's your turn!" The doctor did a general check-up. She listened to his heart and his lungs, and she checked his blood vessels and his backbone too. "Good news — it's probably nothing serious," she said. "It's just a bad cold. You are supposed to take the MEdicine — a cold medicine — three times a day after meals. You should also drink warm water, gargle with salt water, and get some rest. Stay off your feet for a day, and you should feel better in no time."

Steve followed the advice. Therefore, he stayed home to rest. His sister, who is really into healthy living, told him to give her herbal tea a try. "Nothing else seems to be doing the job," Steve said, so he drank it four times a day. As a result, he got rid of the cold. After a few days, he felt healthy again.

Steve's family also had a difficult week. His brother ate spoiled food at a night market and got food poisoning — he had a terrible STOmachache and was vomiting all night. His stomach needed two days of rest, but the pain was getting better on its own. His father loves fried and spicy food, so he had heat in the body and indiGEStion; the doctor said his liver and his kidneys needed a break. His grandmother had constiPAtion, and she felt dizzy when she stood up too quickly. Finally, his little cousin had an ALlergy: itchy skin, a skin rash, and even shortness of breath. The doctor told her to avoid allergens, take allergy pills, and put ice on the itchy skin. What a week!

Part 1 · C

Pronunciation Focus: Word Stress

In English, one syllable in each word is stronger. Wrong stress can make health words hard to understand.

Rule 1 — Most 2-syllable nouns: stress the FIRST syllable

FEver /ˈfiː.vər/ · STOmach /ˈstʌm.ək/ · LIver /ˈlɪv.ər/ · KIDneys /ˈkɪd.niːz/ · PAtient /ˈpeɪ.ʃənt/
🗣 Say it: "The PAtient has a FEver and a STOmachache."

Rule 2 — Words ending in -tion stress the syllable BEFORE it

indiGEStion /ˌɪn.dɪˈdʒes.tʃən/ · constiPAtion /ˌkɑːn.stəˈpeɪ.ʃən/ · prescrIPtion
🗣 Say it: "He has indiGEStion, and his grandmother has constiPAtion."

Rule 3 — Watch these tricky ones

ALlergy /ˈæl.ə.dʒi/ (NOT al-LER-gy) · MEdicine /ˈmed.ɪ.sən/ (often 2 syllables: "MED-sin") · VOmiting /ˈvɒm.ɪ.tɪŋ/
🗣 Say it: "Take your MEdicine — it helps with the ALlergy."

Part 24-Level Progression

Speaking

Speak out loud at every level. Start guided, finish independent. Grammar focus: past simple, "should" for advice, linking words.

🗣 How to practice: Say every answer OUT LOUD before you reveal the model. Speaking silently in your head does not count!
1
Simple Questions
Answer with a body part from A.1. Speak in a full sentence: "My …" or "The … is in trouble."
Q1. If you work on the computer for 10 hours without resting, which organ will complain?
My brain will complain — I will probably get a headache.
Q2. When you go out in the cold winter without a scarf and get a sore throat — which part of the body is in trouble?
My throat is in trouble, and my lungs can suffer too.
Q3. If you eat spoiled food at a night market, where will you feel the pain?
I will feel the pain in my stomach — I will have a stomachache and maybe vomiting.
Q4. Your friend loves eating instant noodles and spicy food every day. Which organ will suffer the most?
His liver will suffer the most — he may get heat in the body and indigestion.
2
Sentence Completion
Speaking Template: "When I have a/an (illness), I have experienced (symptoms). I should (treatment)." Complete each sentence out loud.
1. When I have a/an flu, I have experienced … . I should … .
2. When I have a/an cold, I have experienced … . I should … .
3. When I have a/an allergy, I have experienced … . I should … .
4. When I have food poisoning, I have experienced … . I should … .
5. When I have indigestion, I have experienced … . I should … .
When I have a flu, I have experienced sudden high fever, body aches, sore throat, and I feel dizzy. I should see a doctor or take the medicine.

4
Independent Speaking
Speak for 30–60 seconds per situation. Template: At first…, However…, So…, Therefore…, As a result… (past simple).
🎤 Situation 1: Talk about a day when you felt sick. (What happened at first? What symptoms? What did you decide to do?)
Yesterday I felt sick. At first, I thought I was just a little tired. However, I started to have a fever in the evening. So I decided to stay home.
🎤 Situation 2: The doctor told you to rest. What did you do, and what was the result?
The doctor told me to rest. Therefore, I stayed off my feet for a day. I stayed home and slept more. As a result, I felt better the next day.
🎤 Situation 3: You want to get rid of a cough. What do you do every day? What happens after a few days?
I want to get rid of this cough. So I drink a lot of warm water. I also go to bed early every day. After a few days, the cough disappeared.
🎤 Situation 4: Talk about someone in your family who is really into taking care of their health.
My younger sister is really into taking care of her health. Therefore, she often drinks warm water every morning.

🍵

Scenario 1 — The Herbal Tea Debate (Giving Advice)

Situation: Your friend Steve has a bad cold. The doctor's medicine doesn't seem to help, and he is scheduled to give an important presentation at work on Friday — in just 3 days. Your mother's herbal tea helped you before, but Steve says "Oh, no thanks" to herbal medicine.
🎯 Goal
Convince Steve to try ONE remedy (herbal tea, more rest, another doctor, or your own idea) — or help him make a different plan for Friday. Explain your reasons.
💬 Useful Language
You should…Give it a try!Believe me…You'll be better in no timestay off your feetget rid of
💡 Hints
What if the tea doesn't work?Can he move the presentation?Is a second doctor a good idea?
Steve, listen — the cold medicine doesn't seem to be doing the job, so you should try something different. Give my mom's herbal tea a try: drink it four times a day, stay off your feet tonight, and gargle with salt water. If you don't feel better by Wednesday, you should see another doctor. Don't worry — it's probably nothing serious, and you'll be ready for Friday in no time!
🔄 Extension Questions
1. In your country, what home remedies do people use for a cold? Do they work?
2. When should you trust a doctor, and when is it OK to try home remedies?
🪞 Reflection
Which phrases did you use to give advice? Was your advice polite and convincing?
🏥

Scenario 2 — A Busy Morning at the Clinic (Problem Solving)

Situation: You wake up with a fever, a headache, and a stuffy nose. You have an important English class at 10:00 AM. The clinic opens at 8:00 AM, but it is always crowded — you must register, get a number, and wait in line, which can take two hours. You cannot decide: go to the clinic, take a day off, or go to class and see a doctor later?
🎯 Goal
Make a decision and explain it in 4–5 sentences. There are good reasons for every choice — what matters is YOUR reasoning.
💬 Useful Language
take a day offmake an appointmentit's probably nothing seriousgetting better on its ownI should… because…
💡 Hints
Can you infect your classmates?Is the fever high or low?Can you make an afternoon appointment?
I think I should take a day off. At first, I wanted to go to class. However, I have a fever, so I can make my classmates sick too. I will call the clinic and make an appointment for the afternoon instead of waiting in line all morning. If it's probably nothing serious, I can rest at home, drink warm water, and let my body get better on its own.
🔄 Extension Questions
1. In your country, do people go to work or school when they are a little sick? Is that good or bad?
2. What can schools or companies do to help sick people rest?
🪞 Reflection
Did you explain your decision with reasons (because / so / therefore)? Could you defend a DIFFERENT choice too?
