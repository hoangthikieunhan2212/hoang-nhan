Your Workplace

Talk about where you work with confidence — the type of job, the environment, the people, your daily routine, the company culture, and what you think about it all. Then compare your past and your present using three powerful structures: used to, be used to, and get used to.

(Ở công ty cũ, cơ sở vật chất khá lỗi thời và không gian làm việc chật hẹp. Nhưng giờ văn phòng mới của tôi vô cùng rộng rãi với trang thiết bị hiện đại nhất.)

3 — People at the Workplace

Model: At my previous job, the older staff were quite rigid and uncooperative towards newcomers. But now, I work with very approachable leaders and chill colleagues.
(Tại nơi làm việc trước, nhân viên cũ khá cứng nhắc với người mới. Nhưng giờ tôi làm việc với những lãnh đạo dễ gần và đồng nghiệp rất thoải mái.)

4 — Daily Routine (lunch break)

Model: In the past, I used to eat lunch at my desk and just grab a quick bite because I was so busy. But nowadays, I am used to having a relaxing one-hour lunch break with my colleagues at the company pantry.
(Trước đây tôi từng ăn trưa tại bàn và ăn qua loa vì quá bận. Nhưng hiện tại tôi đã quen với việc có 1 tiếng nghỉ trưa thư giãn cùng đồng nghiệp ở phòng ăn công ty.)

5 — Company Culture (dress code)

Model: At my previous job, they were very strict and we used to dress up in formal business attire every day. But nowadays, I am used to wearing smart-casual clothes because the culture here is so flexible.
(Ở công ty cũ họ rất nghiêm khắc và chúng tôi từng phải mặc đồ công sở trang trọng mỗi ngày. Nhưng giờ tôi đã quen mặc đồ smart-casual vì văn hóa ở đây rất linh hoạt.)

Company

To be honest, switching to a hybrid work model is one of the best changes in my career. I still work for the same company, but a few months ago they completely changed the way we work. Instead of a permanent, five-day, on-site arrangement, the company decided to adopt hybrid work. My contract is still full-time — it's not a part-time job, a temporary job, or freelance work — only the mode has changed. (Thành thật mà nói, chuyển sang mô hình hybrid là một trong những thay đổi tuyệt vời nhất trong sự nghiệp của tôi. Hợp đồng của tôi vẫn là toàn thời gian — chỉ có chế độ làm việc thay đổi.)

Office

In the past, I used to go to the office from Monday to Friday. The open-plan office was quite well-equipped and spacious, with state-of-the-art equipment. But my first office was the opposite: outdated, cramped, and honestly a bit of a toxic workplace. Things are different now — I only go in once or twice a week for important meetings, and otherwise I work from home. (Trước đây tôi từng đến văn phòng từ thứ Hai đến thứ Sáu. Văn phòng mở khá tiện nghi và rộng rãi. Nhưng văn phòng đầu tiên của tôi thì ngược lại: lỗi thời, chật hẹp và khá độc hại.)

People

Even though we don't see each other in person often, my colleagues and line managers always stay in touch. Everyone is competent, open-minded and chill. Nobody is micromanaging, nobody is two-faced or arrogant, and nobody tries to shift blame. From my perspective, that is the biggest difference. (Mặc dù không gặp nhau trực tiếp thường xuyên, đồng nghiệp và quản lý luôn giữ liên lạc. Mọi người đều có năng lực, cởi mở và thoải mái.)

Daily Routine

My routine has become much more flexible. I don't have to wake up super early to fight traffic. Nowadays I am used to starting my day in a relaxing way. I usually take a lunch break at 12 PM. If I work from home, I bring a packed lunch — well, I make it myself. On office days, my team loves to go out for lunch together on Fridays. If a client calls at noon, I just say: "I'm on my lunch break right now, can I call you back?" (Lịch sinh hoạt của tôi linh hoạt hơn nhiều. Tôi thường nghỉ trưa lúc 12 giờ. Ngày lên văn phòng, cả nhóm thích ra ngoài ăn trưa cùng nhau vào thứ Sáu.)

Culture

The company truly trusts its employees and the culture is flat and flexible. My old company had a strict dress code — formal business attire every single day — and older staff used to shift blame and treat newcomers unfairly. Here, I am used to wearing smart-casual clothes, and on Fridays you can basically wear whatever you want. The job is still fast-paced, but nobody cares where you work as long as you meet deadlines. (Công ty thực sự tin tưởng nhân viên và văn hóa rất bình đẳng. Công ty cũ có quy định trang phục nghiêm ngặt. Ở đây tôi đã quen mặc đồ smart-casual.)

Opinion

To be fair, it took me a while to get used to this hybrid way of working. But now I can't imagine going back to the office five days a week. I definitely prefer a flat culture over a high salary. Looking back, leaving my old job was the best choice, and I truly appreciate the freedom I have now. (Thành thật mà nói, tôi mất một thời gian để thích nghi với lối làm việc hybrid này. Nhưng giờ tôi không thể tưởng tượng quay lại văn phòng 5 ngày một tuần. Nhìn lại thì, nghỉ việc ở công ty cũ là lựa chọn tốt nhất.)

Part 1CGrammar Focus

Used to · Be used to · Get used to

Three structures that look almost the same but mean three very different things. Master these and you can compare your past workplace with your present one.

The Big Idea

One story, three moments

Think of moving to a new house.

🏠 USED TO → "I used to live in the countryside." — the old house. Finished. Over.
🚚 GET USED TO → "It took me a month to get used to the traffic noise." — the moving-in period. Difficult at first, easier every day.
🛋️ BE USED TO → "Now I am used to the noise. I sleep fine." — today. It's normal. No problem.

(USED TO = quá khứ đã kết thúc · GET USED TO = đang thích nghi · BE USED TO = đã quen rồi)
⚠️ The trap: After used to → bare verb (go). After be/get used to → -ing or a noun (going / the noise). The word to here is a preposition, not part of an infinitive.

Pronunciation Focus

Part 1D

Connected Speech & Word Stress

Three rules. Practise each one out loud five times.

Rule 1 — "used to" becomes /ˈjuːstə/

Never say "u-sed tooo". The d disappears and to becomes a weak /tə/. All three structures do this.

used to go sounds like /ˈjuːstə ɡəʊ/

I used to work on-site. → /aɪ ˈjuːstə wɜːk/
I'm used to working from home. → /aɪm ˈjuːstə ˈwɜːkɪŋ/
I'll get used to it. → /aɪl ɡet ˈjuːstə ɪt/

Rule 2 — Linking consonant → vowel

When a word ends in a consonant and the next starts with a vowel, they glue together into one sound.

pick up sounds like /pɪˈkʌp/

take a lunch break → /teɪ‿kə lʌntʃ breɪk/
go out for lunch → /ɡəʊ‿aʊt fə lʌntʃ/
an open-minded boss → /ə‿nəʊpən ˈmaɪndɪd/

Rule 3 — Stress in compound adjectives

Workplace adjectives are often two or three words joined by hyphens. The stress usually falls on the last part.

state-of-the-ART stress on ART

well-eQUIPPED · fast-PACED · open-MINDed
user-FRIENDly · out-of-DATE · two-FACED
⚠️ Exception: MIcromanaging (stress at the start, like the prefix micro-).
