A Week of Weather

My friend Minh studies in the United Kingdom — a country with a TEMperate climate: four clear seasons. In spring it is warm, and the sky is often blue; in summer it can be hot and sunny; in autumn (Americans say fall) it is cool and windy, and the mornings are chilly; and in winter it is cold — sometimes freezing, with snowy mornings, icy roads, and thick fog that makes driving dangerous.

Yesterday Minh video-called me. "What's the weather like in Ho Chi Minh City? What's it like outside today?" I laughed: "It's really hot and humid today. There is no wind." Vietnam has a TROpical climate — hot all year, with a rainy season and a dry season, and in April the city can be boiling, nearly 40°C. My cousin lives in South Korea, which is subTROpical: HUmid, hot summers but quite cold winters.

Then, this afternoon, the weather changed fast. First the air became extremely humid. Suddenly we heard THunder and saw lightning — a real thunderstorm! It began to pour with rain: heavy rain, not a small shower. A strong wind (never say "big wind"!) shook the trees — the wind has blown all the leaves off our mango tree. My mother asked from the kitchen: "Is it still raining?" An hour later, only light showers were left. Then the sun came out, and by dinner time the sky was clear — not one cloudy corner, no foggy air, just a beautiful evening.

After dinner I watched a world weather report, just like the one you will hear in Part 3. Beijing was cold and windy, with a low of 0°C. New York was even colder — freezing at minus ten! Tokyo was cloudy and rainy, Mexico City was warm and wet, and Taipei was hot, cloudy and wet at 30°C.

This week the seasons are changing here too. It's getting warmer every day; the temperature is going up every hour in the afternoon, and going down only after the sun has set. My classmates are planning a picnic on Saturday — but the sky is getting dark, and the wind is getting stronger. Should we go anyway, move the party indoors, or wait for Sunday? In Part 4, you will help us decide.

Part 1 · B

Useful Phrases

Three core structures for weather talk + word partners that make you sound natural.

🧩 3 structures to remember: 1) Asking · 2) Describing current weather (It is + adjective) · 3) Describing change (It's getting + -er / the temperature is going up/down)
🤝 Word partners = words that always travel together. Say thick fog (not "bad fog"), strong wind (not "big wind"), heavy rain (not "big rain").
Part 1 · C

Pronunciation Focus: Word Stress & the /θ/ Sound

Weather words hide two traps for Vietnamese speakers: stress position and the "th" sound.

Rule 1 — Climate words: stress falls on TROP / TEM

TROpical /ˈtrɒp.ɪ.kəl/ · subTROpical /ˌsʌbˈtrɒp.ɪ.kəl/ · TEMperate /ˈtem.pər.ət/ (NOT "tem-per-ATE")
🗣 Say it: "Vietnam is TROpical, Korea is subTROpical, and the UK is TEMperate."

Rule 2 — -y adjectives end with a light /i/ sound

sunny /ˈsʌn.i/ · windy /ˈwɪn.di/ · cloudy /ˈklaʊ.di/ · foggy /ˈfɒɡ.i/ · icy /ˈaɪ.si/ · chilly /ˈtʃɪl.i/
🗣 Say it: "It's windy, cloudy, and a little chilly today."

Rule 3 — The /θ/ sound: tongue between your teeth

thunder /ˈθʌn.dər/ · thick fog /θɪk/ · weather /ˈweð.ər/ (this one is the soft /ð/!)
🗣 Say it: "I heard thunder — thick clouds are coming. What's the weather like now?"

Speaking

Tab 2A: guided speaking in 4 levels. Tab 2B: reflex training — draw, speak, beat the clock.

🗣 How to practice: Say every answer OUT LOUD before you reveal the model. Speaking silently in your head does not count!
1
Simple Questions
Answer in a full sentence using today's vocabulary.
Q1. What's the weather like today in your city?
It's really hot and humid today, and there is no wind.
Q2. Which season do you like best? Why?
I like autumn best because it is cool and the sky is often clear.
Q3. What climate type does Vietnam have? Describe it.
Vietnam has a tropical climate — it is hot all year, with a rainy season and a dry season.
Q4. Is it getting hotter or cooler in your city these days?
It's getting hotter — the temperature is going up every day.

Useful: humid · thunder · lightning · pour with rain · It's getting…

4
Independent Speaking
Speak for 30–60 seconds per situation. Use the structures: It is + adjective / It's getting + -er / The temperature is going up (down).
🎤 Situation 1: Describe a thunderstorm from beginning to end — what happens first, next, and after?
First, the air becomes very humid. Then you hear thunder and see lightning. Right after that, heavy rain pours down. Later, the light showers stop, the sun comes out, and the sky is clear again.
🎤 Situation 2: Compare the weather in two seasons in your country.
In the dry season it is hot and sunny, and the temperature can go up to 38°C — sometimes it's boiling. In the rainy season it is still warm, but there are heavy showers almost every afternoon, and the air is very humid.
🎤 Situation 3: Compare a tropical climate and a temperate climate.
A tropical climate, like in Vietnam, is hot all year with a rainy season and a dry season. A temperate climate, like in the UK, has four clear seasons: warm summers and cold winters — sometimes freezing, with snowy and icy days.
🎤 Situation 4: The weather is changing right now outside your window. Describe the change.
The sky is getting darker and the wind is getting stronger. The temperature is going down quickly. I think it's going to rain — I can already see thick clouds.

Part 4Free Communication

Discussion

There is no single correct answer here. Use today's language to solve real situations — think, decide, and explain why.

🧺

Scenario 1 — The Saturday Picnic Problem (Decision Making)

Situation: Your class planned a picnic in the park this Saturday. But today the sky is getting dark and cloudy, the wind is getting stronger, and the air feels extremely humid — a thunderstorm may be coming. The weather report says: heavy showers on Saturday morning, but the sun may come out in the afternoon. Ten classmates already bought food.
🎯 Goal
Decide as a group: go anyway in the afternoon, move the picnic indoors, or postpone to Sunday. Explain your choice with weather reasons.
💬 Useful Language
It's getting…The report says…heavy showers / light showersThe sun may come outI think we should… because…
💡 Hints
What if it pours at lunch time?Is the food easy to move indoors?Will everyone be free on Sunday?
I think we should move the picnic to Saturday afternoon, not cancel it. The report says heavy showers in the morning, but the sun may come out later. If the sky is still dark at noon and we hear thunder, we should move everything indoors — nobody wants to eat in pouring rain! Sunday is a backup, but not everyone is free.
🔄 Extension Questions
1. In your country, which months are best for outdoor plans? Why?
2. Have you ever had a plan destroyed by the weather? What happened?
🪞 Reflection
Did you give weather reasons for your decision (because / so / the report says…)? Could you defend a different choice too?
🧳

Scenario 2 — Advising a Traveler (Giving Advice)

Situation: Your friend Minh from the UK (temperate climate) is visiting Vietnam (tropical) in July for the first time. He asks: "What's the weather like there in July? What should I pack?" He is used to cool summers around 20°C and has never seen a tropical thunderstorm.
🎯 Goal
Describe July weather in Vietnam and give him a packing list of at least 3 items — with weather reasons for each.
💬 Useful Language
It's really hot and humidboiling — up to 38°Cheavy showers in the afternoonYou should bring… because…tropical vs temperate
💡 Hints
Umbrella or raincoat?What about the strong sun?Warn him about thunderstorms
July in Vietnam is very different from the UK — it's the rainy season, so it's really hot and humid, and the temperature can go up to 38°C. You should bring light clothes because it's boiling in the afternoon, an umbrella because there are heavy showers almost every day, and sunglasses for the strong sun in the morning. Don't worry about thunderstorms — they pour with rain for an hour, then the sun comes out and the sky is clear again!
🔄 Extension Questions
1. What would YOU need to pack for a winter trip to a temperate country?
2. Which climate would you prefer to live in — tropical, subtropical, or temperate? Why?
🪞 Reflection
Did you connect every item to a weather reason? Did you use comparison words (hotter, more humid than…)?
